Shared Support Resources

These materials are part of a publicly shared collection of practical support and learning resources used by families, educators, peer supporters, and community members.

They are independently developed and freely accessible so they can be used without cost, registration, or coordination.

If you arrived here before seeing an example, this page explains what the materials are and why they are shared.

What These Resources Help With

These materials are designed to help ordinary people handle difficult real-life human situations more safely and calmly.

They are especially used when someone notices a person who may be overwhelmed, withdrawn, distressed, or struggling but does not know how to approach them without making things worse.

The goal is not counseling and not intervention.

The goal is:

  • noticing early signs
  • responding gently
  • avoiding escalation
  • knowing when to step back
  • helping without pressure or harm

They function as a small guided rehearsal for real-world moments that families, educators, peer supporters, and community members regularly encounter.

Why They Are Freely Shared

The materials are maintained by an individual living with a disability. Because traditional payments can interfere with disability benefits, they are shared publicly instead of sold.

Support, when people choose to give it, is handled separately through a disability savings program and is never required to use the materials.
